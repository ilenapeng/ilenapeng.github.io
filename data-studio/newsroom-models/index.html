Digital Native Local Newsrooms and the Nonprofit Model

More local outlets are operating under the umbrella of nonprofit organizations

By Ilena Peng

April 24, 2022

The number of American digital native local newsrooms operating under the umbrella of a nonprofit have increased in the past decade, according to data from Project Oasis, an initiative including groups like the UNC Hussman School of Journalism and Media and the Google News Initiative.

Digital native local news outlets founded under a nonprofit parent organization were first recorded by Project Oasis in 2009, just four years after the nonprofit newsroom model appeared in the initiativeâ€™s dataset. Since then, they have become a more common structure. In 2020, four digital native local newsrooms were founded under the umbrella of a nonprofit, but none were founded as a standalone nonprofit.

Project Oasis, which compiles its data using publicly available information and self-reported information from newsrooms, has identified a total of 25 digital native local news publishers that were created under a nonprofit umbrella and 32 that are nonprofits.

Nonprofit organizations, which are supported through means like individual memberships, donations and grants, have become an increasingly popular model among local newsrooms. Operating under the umbrella of a larger non-profit organization allows the smaller entity to receive some of the same tax benefits, without the sometimes long wait for nonprofit status approval.
